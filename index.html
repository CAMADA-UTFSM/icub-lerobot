iCub-LeRobot: A VR Teleoperation Framework for Scalable VLA Dataset Generation

Miguel Rojas1, Mauricio Araya1,
1Department of Electronic Engineering, Universidad Técnica Federico Santa María, Valparaíso, Chile
2026 IEEE International Conference on Development and Learning (ICDL)
Code Dataset OpenReview

Abstract

The emergence of autonomous skills in humanoid robots requires a deep integration of multimodal sensory streams and motor control. We present iCub-LeRobot, an open-source framework designed to advance cognitive developmental robotics by bridging the iCub humanoid with the modern LeRobot ecosystem. While the architecture is designed to support MuJoCo, Gazebo, and real-world environments, we validate our pipeline through a single-arm proof-of-concept in MuJoCo. By providing a low-cost VR teleoperation interface, our system enables the generation of contact-rich, action-contingent datasets. The framework seamlessly captures proprioception, active gaze, and tactile feedback, formatting them directly for training state-of-the-art Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models. By acting as a bridge between human instructional scaffolding and foundation models, iCub-LeRobot provides the cognitive robotics community with the foundational tools to study sensorimotor learning and the future emergence of complex bimanual skills on the iCub robot.

Overview of the iCub-LeRobot system architecture for human-guided sensorimotor learning. The framework establishes a bidirectional, low-latency communication bridge (ZeroMQ) between the Meta Quest 2 VR interface and the robot execution layer. A shared feature space allows for seamless switching between the MuJoCo digital twin and the physical iCub via YARP. The synchronized multimodal streams (proprioception, vision, and tactile feedback) are natively formatted into the LeRobot dataset ecosystem for training foundational Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models.

Pipeline verification showcasing high-frequency bimanual telemetry logging during human-guided teleoperation in MuJoCo, alongside the final evaluated results of our end-to-end learning architecture.

Teleoperation of the physical iCub robot using the Meta Quest 2 VR interface.
